When Ringo Starr replaced Pete Best in 1962, many Beatles fans were furious. They didn’t see the missing piece. They saw an outsider sitting behind the drums. Some shouted for Pete. Some refused to accept Ringo. One angry fan went further — and George Harrison got punched for standing by Ringo. Sixteen months later, George and Ringo were at Alpha Studios in Aston, Birmingham, as The Beatles closed Thank Your Lucky Stars with She Loves You. By then, the doubt had vanished. The bruises were gone. Britain was screaming. America was next.
